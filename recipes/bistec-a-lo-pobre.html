Bistec a Lo Pobre

Description

Bistec a lo Pobre is a popular dish in South America, though more commonly found in Chile and Peru. The name can be roughly translated to “poor man's steak,” even though the flavors are very rich and decadent. Even though the exact origin is unknown, there are a few theories of the meal's history. The leading theory is that many working-class citizens ate the meal since it has many proteins and carbohydrates. Since the meal only had a few ingredients, it was also ideal for lower-paid workers.

Ingredients

  • 4 Thinly Cut Steaks
  • 3 Onions
  • 4 Eggs
  • 4 Potatoes
  • Oil
  • 4 Garlic Cloves
  • Salt and Pepper

Steps

  1. Peel all of the potatoes then cut them into fries. Soak your fries for 30 minutes and dry well before frying in hot oil at 360 ℉ (182.22°C). Remove the fries when they are golden brown and keep them warm in the oven at 200 ℉ (93.33°C).
  2. Cook the onions in oil, sautéing them until they are a golden brown color. They should also be soft. After they finish cooking, keep them warm in the oven.
  3. Season the steak with garlic and salt, and pepper. If you have a meat hammer, flatten the steaks so they're all the same size.
  4. Cook the meat over high heat. Depending on their thickness, this could mean anywhere from three to five minutes per side.
  5. Fry the eggs in oil. The eggs are traditionally served sunny side up (with a runny yolk).
  6. Put some onions on your plate, and then some fries. Place the steak over top of the onions and fries. Finally, top the steak with the fried egg.
