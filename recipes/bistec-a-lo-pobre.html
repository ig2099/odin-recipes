Bistec a Lo Pobre

Description

Bistec a lo Pobre is a popular dish in South America, though more commonly found in Chile and Peru. The name can be roughly translated to “poor man's steak,” even though the flavors are very rich and decadent. Even though the exact origin is unknown, there are a few theories of the meal's history. The leading theory is that many working-class citizens ate the meal since it has many proteins and carbohydrates. Since the meal only had a few ingredients, it was also ideal for lower-paid workers.

Ingredients

  • 4 Thinly Cut Steaks
  • 3 Onions
  • 4 Eggs
  • 4 Potatoes
  • Oil
  • 4 Garlic Cloves
  • Salt and Pepper
